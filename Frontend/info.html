Pneumonia:

General Information

  • Pneumonia is an infection of the lungs that causes the air sacs (alveoli) to fill with fluid or pus.
  • It can affect one or both lungs.
  • It is common and can range from mild to serious.
  • People at higher risk include young children, older adults, and people with weaker immune systems.

Causes (Medical)

  • Bacteria (e.g., Streptococcus pneumoniae)
  • Viruses (e.g., influenza, COVID-19)
  • Fungi (more common in people with weakened immunity)

Symptoms

  • Cough (often with mucus)
  • Fever and chills
  • Shortness of breath
  • Chest pain when breathing or coughing
  • Fatigue
  • Rapid breathing or heartbeat

Diagnosis (Doctors may use)

  • Chest X-ray
  • Blood tests
  • Oxygen level measurement
  • Sputum (mucus) test

Treatment

  • Bacterial pneumonia → antibiotics
  • Viral pneumonia → supportive care (rest, fluids)
  • Severe cases may require hospital care and oxygen

Preventions

  • Vaccination (flu vaccine, pneumococcal vaccine)
  • Handwashing
  • Avoiding smoking
  • Covering coughs and sneezes

Tuberculosis(TB):

General Information

  • Tuberculosis (TB) is a serious infectious disease caused by a bacterium.
  • It mainly affects the lungs, but can also affect the brain, kidneys, or spine.
  • TB spreads through the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes.
  • TB develops slowly compared to pneumonia.

Causes (Medical)

  • Caused by the bacterium Mycobacterium tuberculosis
  • There are two forms:
    • Latent TB – bacteria present but no symptoms and not contagious
    • Active TB – symptoms present and contagious

Symptoms

  • Persistent cough (lasting weeks)
  • Fever
  • Night sweats
  • Weight loss
  • Fatigue
  • Chest pain

Diagnosis (Doctors may use)

  • TB skin test or blood test
  • Chest X-ray
  • Sputum test for TB bacteria

Treatment

  • Treated with specific antibiotics taken for several months
  • Completing the full treatment is very important to prevent drug resistance

Preventions

  • Early detection and treatment
  • Wearing masks in high-risk settings
  • TB vaccination (BCG) in some countries
